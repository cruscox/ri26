Why Bangkok Expats Choose a Hotel Over a Serviced Apartment

Bangkok's expat community has always maintained a spectrum of accommodation preferences. Serviced apartments offer independent living; hotels offer services. The question is which package better matches the individual's Bangkok lifestyle — and for many, particularly those on shorter long-stays (1-3 months), mid-term work trips, or those between apartments, a quality Bangkok hotel with monthly rates wins on value and convenience.

Royal Ivory Nana Hotel has a long-standing expat clientele for good reason. The Deluxe kitchenette rooms (42+sqm) provide semi-independent living. The no joiner charge policy, outdoor pool, breakfast inclusion, full Thai restaurant, and discreet professional staff address the practical needs of long-term foreign residents. And the Sukhumvit Soi 4 location — central BTS access, quiet side street, everything within reach — is one of Bangkok's most liveable.

Kitchenette Rooms — Semi-Independent Living

The Deluxe room at Royal Ivory (42+sqm, from 1,800 THB/night) includes a kitchenette: refrigerator, microwave, and kettle. This covers the most important independent living needs — storing food and drink, reheating meals, making tea and coffee at any hour — without the inconvenience of a full apartment kitchen.

Combined with the included daily breakfast and on-site Thai restaurant, most expats find the kitchenette handles their daily needs comfortably. Supermarkets on Sukhumvit (Tops, Villa Market, Gourmet Market at EmQuartier 2 BTS stops away) stock Western and international groceries for any self-catering preferences.

Bumrungrad — Walking Distance

Bumrungrad International Hospital — one of Asia's most respected medical facilities, popular with both expats and medical tourists — is a 10-minute walk from Royal Ivory on Sukhumvit Soi 3. For expats managing ongoing medical care, annual checkups, or dental work, the hospital's proximity is a meaningful practical advantage over many other Bangkok neighbourhoods.

Royal Ivory Nana Hotel negotiates monthly rates for long-term guests. Monthly pricing is not published — it is agreed directly based on room type, length of stay, season, and current availability. The hotel's approach is flexible: they work with expats and long-stay guests to find rates that work.
