Top Bangkok Attractions from Royal IvoryDiscover Bangkok's temples, markets, culture, and experiences from our prime Nana location

Benjakitti Park 300 meters awayPeaceful green space right next door - 3 minute walk

Benjakitti Park (also known as Benjakiti Park) is a beautiful 130-acre public park built on land donated by the Tobacco Authority of Thailand to Queen Sirikit in 1992. The park features a large lake perfect for jogging, cycling, and relaxing. It's the closest attraction to Royal Ivory Nana Hotel.

Key Information:

  • Distance from Royal Ivory: Just 300 meters (3 minute walk)
  • Entrance: FREE admission
  • Opening Hours: 05:00 - 20:00 daily
  • Activities: Jogging track (2km), cycling path, paddle boats, outdoor gym
  • Perfect for: Morning exercise, evening walks, photography

From Royal Ivory: Walk straight out to Sukhumvit Road, turn left, park entrance is 300 meters ahead. Perfect for morning jogs or evening relaxation.

Directions: Royal Ivory to Benjakitti Park Learn More

Terminal 21 1km awayIconic airport-themed shopping mall - 12 minute walk

Terminal 21 is Bangkok's unique airport-themed shopping mall where each floor represents a different world city - Tokyo, London, Istanbul, San Francisco, Paris, and more. Opened in 2011, it's become one of Bangkok's most popular shopping destinations combining affordable shopping with stunning themed decor.

Key Information:

  • Distance: 1km from Royal Ivory (12 minute walk or 1 BTS stop to Asok)
  • Opening Hours: 10:00 - 22:00 daily
  • Floors: 9 floors of shopping + Pier 21 Food Court on 5th floor
  • Shops: Fashion, electronics, cosmetics, souvenirs (200+ stores)
  • Famous for: Budget-friendly prices, Instagram-worthy interiors

From Royal Ivory: Walk 12 minutes along Sukhumvit Road towards Asok, or take BTS one stop from Nana to Asok station (connected to mall).

Directions: Royal Ivory to Terminal 21 Learn More

Grand Palace 6km awayBangkok's #1 must-see attraction - Home of Emerald Buddha

The Grand Palace is Bangkok's most famous landmark and a must-visit for every tourist. Built in 1782, it served as the official residence of Thai Kings for 150 years. The palace complex covers 218,400 square meters and includes Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of the Emerald Buddha), Thailand's most sacred Buddhist temple.

Key Information:

  • #1 Attraction in Bangkok - Visited by millions yearly
  • Entrance Fee: 500 Baht (includes audio guide)
  • Opening Hours: 08:30 - 15:30 daily (last entry 15:00)
  • Dress Code: Strictly enforced - Cover shoulders and knees, no sandals
  • Must-see: Emerald Buddha statue, golden spires, intricate architecture

From Royal Ivory: Take BTS to Saphan Taksin, then express boat to Tha Chang pier (40 minutes total), or taxi/Grab (20-30 minutes depending on traffic).

Directions: Royal Ivory to Grand Palace Learn More

Asiatique The Riverfront 8km awayPopular riverside night market and entertainment complex

Asiatique The Riverfront opened in 2012 on the banks of the Chao Phraya River. Built on the former docks of the East Asiatic Company, this open-air mall combines 1,500 boutiques and 40 restaurants with entertainment venues including Calypso Cabaret and Muay Thai Live shows.

Key Information:

  • Opening Hours: 17:00 - 24:00 daily (evening/night market)
  • Entrance: FREE (pay only for what you buy)
  • Attractions: Shopping, dining, Asiatique Sky (60m Ferris wheel)
  • Shows: Calypso Cabaret, Muay Thai Live, puppet shows
  • Perfect for: Evening entertainment, riverside dining, souvenir shopping

From Royal Ivory: Take BTS to Saphan Taksin station, then free shuttle boat directly to Asiatique (boats every 15-20 minutes). Total journey 35-40 minutes.

Directions: Royal Ivory to Asiatique Learn More

Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn) 2.9km awayWorld-renowned iconic riverside temple

Built in the 18th century, Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn) is best enjoyed at sunrise when the first light illuminates its magnificent spires. The temple's highlight is its impressive 80-meter central prang (tower), adorned with intricate pagodas and decorated with colorful porcelain and seashells.

Key Information:

  • TripAdvisor Ranking: #4 attraction in Bangkok (2012)
  • Entrance Fee: 50 Baht
  • Opening Hours: 08:30 - 17:30 daily
  • Architecture: Khmer-style tower with Thai decorative elements
  • Location: West bank of Chao Phraya River

From Royal Ivory: Take BTS to Saphan Taksin, then express boat to Wat Arun pier (approximately 30 minutes total).

Directions: Royal Ivory to Wat Arun Learn More

Damnoen Saduak Floating Market 100km awayThailand's most famous floating market with 1,000s of vendors

The Damnoen Saduak canal was constructed in 1866 under the commission of King Rama IV, initially serving as a vital transportation route. Today, it has evolved into Thailand's most successful floating market, attracting visitors from around the world while preserving its authentic Thai character.

Market Experience:

  • Vendors sell directly from traditional wooden boats
  • Fresh tropical fruits, local food, handicrafts, and souvenirs
  • Authentic Thai cultural experience
  • Photo opportunities with traditional Thai boats
  • Sample local delicacies and Thai sweets

Visitor Information:

  • Open Daily: 07:30 - 12:30 (arrive early for best experience)
  • Distance: 100km southwest of Bangkok
  • Best visited as a morning trip
  • Royal Ivory can arrange transportation
Directions: Royal Ivory to Floating Market Learn More

Chatuchak Weekend Market 10km awayOne of the world's largest weekend markets with 15,000+ stalls

Established in 1958 as Phahonyothin market, it was renamed Chatuchak Market in 1987 after relocating near Chatuchak Park. This massive market is one of the world's largest weekend markets, featuring over 15,000 stalls spread across 27 acres.

What You'll Find:

  • Clothing and fashion accessories
  • Antiques and collectibles
  • Home dÃ©cor and furniture
  • Plants and gardening supplies
  • Pets and pet supplies
  • Art and handicrafts
  • Books and music
  • Delicious Thai street food

Getting There:

  • BTS Skytrain: Mo Chit Station (Northern Line)
  • MRT Subway: Chatuchak Park Station or Kamphaeng Phet Station
  • From Royal Ivory: BTS Nana to Mo Chit (30 minutes)
  • Open: Saturdays and Sundays, 09:00 - 18:00

Pro Tip: Visit early morning (09:00) to avoid crowds and heat. Wear comfortable shoes and bring cash (many vendors don't accept cards).

Directions: Royal Ivory to Chatuchak Learn More

Lumpini Boxing Stadium 3km awayExperience authentic Muay Thai boxing matches

Witness the ancient art of Muay Thai (Thai kickboxing) at the legendary Lumpini Boxing Stadium. Muay Thai dates back to medieval times and was famously practiced by King Naresuan. Today, it captivates audiences worldwide with its powerful strikes, graceful movements, and rich cultural tradition.

About Muay Thai:

  • Ancient martial art, now Thailand's national sport
  • Uses fists, elbows, knees, and shins
  • Traditional Wai Kru ceremony before fights
  • Live traditional music during matches
  • Authentic Thai sporting experience

Stadium Information:

  • TripAdvisor Rank: #32 in Bangkok attractions
  • Fight Nights: Tuesday, Friday, Saturday
  • Ticket Prices: 1,000 - 2,000 Baht (depending on seat)
  • Duration: Matches run from approximately 18:30 - 23:00

Getting There: Just 1 MRT subway station from Royal Ivory Hotel - take MRT from Sukhumvit Station to Lumpini Station (approximately 5 minutes). The stadium is a short walk from the station.

Directions: Royal Ivory to Lumpini Stadium Learn More

Chinatown (Yaowarat) 7km awayHistoric district with authentic street food and shopping

Yaowarat, Bangkok's famous Chinatown, originated in the late 17th century as a pier for Chinese immigrants. Today, it maintains its historic charm while serving as a major commercial hub and one of Bangkok's most vibrant food destinations.

What Makes Yaowarat Special:

  • Historic architecture and traditional shophouses
  • World-famous street food scene
  • Gold shops and traditional Chinese medicine
  • Wholesale fashion and electronics
  • Authentic bargaining culture

Shopping Tips:

  • Bargaining is expected - start at 50% of asking price
  • Many wholesale goods available at great prices
  • Best time: Late afternoon to evening (15:00 onwards)
  • The street truly comes alive at night

Must-Try Foods:

  • Guay Jub (rolled rice noodles)
  • Fresh seafood at street stalls
  • Dim sum and dumplings
  • Mango sticky rice
  • Traditional Chinese pastries

Getting There: TripAdvisor Rank #38 in Bangkok. From Royal Ivory: 7km west (15 minutes by motorcycle taxi, 20 minutes by regular taxi depending on traffic). MRT to Hua Lamphong Station, then short taxi ride or walk.

Directions: Royal Ivory to Chinatown Learn More
