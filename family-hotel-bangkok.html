Why Room Size Is the First Priority for Family Hotels

Bangkok's budget hotel market is dominated by small rooms. The standard budget double in Sukhumvit averages 15-22 sqm — workable for one person, cramped for two, and genuinely uncomfortable for a family of three or four. Finding a family-suited hotel at Bangkok's 3-star budget price point requires specifically looking for properties with larger-than-average rooms.

Royal Ivory Nana Hotel's smallest room is 32 sqm — already significantly larger than the market average. The Deluxe at 42+ sqm and our largest rooms at 45 sqm with a separate living area provide genuine family living space. Combined with an outdoor pool, included breakfast for all guests, a full on-site restaurant, and a quiet Soi 4 location with excellent BTS access, Royal Ivory is a practical Bangkok family base.

Room Options for Families

Deluxe Room — 42+ sqm (Best for Small Families)

The Deluxe is the recommended room for families of 2-3. At 42+ sqm, there's genuine living space — room for the children to move around, unpack, and not feel on top of each other. The kitchenette (refrigerator, microwave, kettle) lets parents store snacks, drinks, and baby supplies — a meaningful practical advantage on a family trip. Air-conditioning throughout. Breakfast included. Pool access included. From 1,800 THB/night.

Suite — 45 sqm (Families of 3-4)

The Suite provides a full apartment-style space at 45 sqm — a separate living room distinct from the bedroom, bathtub in addition to shower, and premium Thai decor. For families of 3-4 guests, the separate living area means parents and children have their own zones. The bathtub is particularly popular for families with young children. From 2,800 THB/night.

Two Superior Rooms — Adjoining Option

For larger families or those who prefer separate sleeping spaces, two Superior rooms (32 sqm each) provide 64 sqm total. Contact the hotel at +66 2 656 7888 to request adjoining or connecting rooms — the team will do their best to accommodate adjacent room bookings for families.

Pool and Food — The Family Essentials

Outdoor Pool — Free for All Guests

The outdoor pool at Royal Ivory is one of the few in the budget 3-star Nana area. For families with children, the pool is often the defining feature of a hotel stay — the place where the whole trip comes together after a day of Bangkok sightseeing. Bangkok's year-round heat (25-35°C) makes pool access valuable every day, not just a luxury amenity. Free for all guests — no additional charge.

Breakfast for All — Included

Breakfast is included in the room rate for all guests — meaning the whole family eats without additional cost. Served in the hotel's Thai restaurant, it's a proper sit-down start to the day that sets the tone for family sightseeing. Not having to organise food for the whole family before leaving the hotel significantly reduces the daily stress of travelling with children.

On-Site Thai Restaurant

The hotel's full Thai restaurant provides a reliable, familiar dining option for families throughout the day. After long days of sightseeing when children are tired and parents don't want to navigate Bangkok's street food scene, having an on-site restaurant with a proper menu is a significant practical convenience.

Location and Safety for Families

Quiet Side Street — Sukhumvit Soi 4

Royal Ivory is on Sukhumvit Soi 4 — a side street that branches off the main Sukhumvit Road. During the day, Soi 4 is a normal, quiet Bangkok street: restaurants, massage shops, convenience stores, and locals going about their business. Families walking to BTS Nana (700m along the soi) will pass by nothing unusual. The adult entertainment venues on Soi 4 operate exclusively in the evening and are closed during daytime sightseeing hours.

BTS Access to All Family Attractions

The BTS Skytrain from Nana station (300m from the hotel) connects families safely and comfortably to Bangkok's major tourist attractions — no tuk-tuk negotiation, no taxi meter disputes, no traffic for most of the journey:

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Royal Ivory Nana Hotel suitable for families with children?

Yes. Royal Ivory Nana Hotel has spacious rooms (32-45sqm) suited for families, an outdoor pool free for all guests, breakfast included for all, and a full Thai restaurant on-site. The Deluxe room (42+sqm with kitchenette) and Suite (45sqm with separate living room) are the most practical for families. BTS Nana is 300m away — safe, air-conditioned transport to all Bangkok family attractions.

What is the best room type for families at Royal Ivory?

For families of 2-3, the Deluxe room (42+sqm, from 1,800 THB) with kitchenette is the best choice — extra space plus refrigerator for storing drinks and snacks. For families of 3-4, the Suite (45sqm, from 2,800 THB) with separate living room and bathtub provides genuine family-apartment comfort. All room types include breakfast and pool access.

Is the Nana area of Bangkok suitable for families?

During the day, the Nana / Sukhumvit Soi 4 area is a normal, busy Bangkok neighbourhood — convenient for shopping, transport, and food. Adult entertainment venues operate exclusively in the evenings and are unremarkable during daytime hours. Families sightseeing with the BTS from Nana Station will have no issues. The hotel's Soi 4 side-street location is quieter than the main Sukhumvit Road.

Are there family attractions near BTS Nana Bangkok?

Yes. From BTS Nana, families can reach: Terminal 21 (1 stop — themed mall, food court, cinema), SEA LIFE Ocean World (4 stops to Siam — aquarium inside Siam Paragon), Chatuchak Weekend Market (BTS to Mo Chit — enormous market), and Grand Palace (BTS + ferry — Bangkok's most iconic sight). All accessible on the same Skytrain line from Nana.
