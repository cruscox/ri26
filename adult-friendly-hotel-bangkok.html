Understanding Bangkok's Joiner Charge — and Why We Don't Have One

What is a Joiner Charge?

A joiner charge (also called a "guest fee" or "overnight visitor fee") is an extra surcharge — typically 500-1,000 Thai Baht per night — that many Bangkok hotels add when guests bring an adult visitor to their room. The charge appears on your bill at checkout, often without clear disclosure at booking.

Joiner charges are particularly common in the Nana and Sukhumvit areas of Bangkok, where hotels recognize that some guests visit with companions from the local entertainment district. The fee is controversial: critics argue it's an unjustified tax on adult privacy, effectively punishing guests for lawful personal choices.

Royal Ivory's Position

Royal Ivory Nana Hotel takes a clear position: we do not charge joiner fees. Our reasoning is simple — guests who have paid for a room have the right to use that room as any adult would in private. Adding arbitrary fees for legal adult activities is disrespectful, discriminatory, and contrary to the spirit of genuine hospitality.

This policy reflects Thai values of nam jai (generosity of spirit) and kreng jai (consideration for others). We extend these values to every guest by treating them as the independent adults they are.

Privacy is also a physical reality. Our Superior rooms (32 sqm), Deluxe rooms (42 sqm+), and Suites (80 sqm) give guests genuine space. Thin walls, cramped rooms, and adjacent noise are the enemy of privacy — our rooms are designed to be comfortable and properly insulated.

No Unnecessary Staff Intrusion

Room service, housekeeping, and maintenance all operate by guest request or pre-scheduled service. Staff do not enter rooms without explicit guest permission. The "Do Not Disturb" sign is respected absolutely. You control your room environment completely.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Royal Ivory Hotel charge a joiner fee?

No. Royal Ivory Nana Hotel has a strict no joiner charge policy. Adult visitors are welcome in guests' rooms with no additional fees of any kind. This policy is firm and applies to all stays and all room types.

Is Royal Ivory Hotel adult-friendly?

Yes. Royal Ivory is widely considered one of Bangkok's most genuinely adult-friendly hotels. "Adult-friendly" means: no joiner charges, no restrictions on legal adult behavior, discreet and professional staff, and a non-judgmental environment. We have maintained this policy since opening and intend to keep it permanently.

How does Royal Ivory protect guest privacy?

Royal Ivory protects guest privacy through: (1) a no joiner charge / no-fees-for-visitors policy, (2) staff trained in discretion and non-judgment, (3) a quiet side-street location away from main-road visibility, (4) strict DND (Do Not Disturb) policy respected by all staff, and (5) no unnecessary sharing of guest information.

Do Bangkok hotels have to charge a joiner fee?

No, joiner charges are not mandatory or legally required in Thailand. They are a discretionary hotel policy. Royal Ivory has chosen to permanently remove joiner charges as a reflection of our commitment to guest freedom and privacy. Guests should always verify a hotel's joiner policy before booking in Bangkok.

What languages does Royal Ivory staff speak?

Royal Ivory staff speak Thai, English, Chinese (Mandarin), Japanese, Korean, and Arabic. All front desk staff are trained to handle international guests with cultural sensitivity and discretion appropriate to each guest's background and preferences.
