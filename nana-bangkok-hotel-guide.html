What is the Nana Area in Bangkok?

When travelers search for a Nana Bangkok hotel, they're looking for accommodation in one of Bangkok's most internationally recognized districts. The Nana area covers the neighborhood surrounding Sukhumvit Sois 4 through 11, centered on BTS Nana Station on the BTS Sukhumvit Line.

Nana is Bangkok's most international and diverse neighborhood. Originally developed as an expatriate hub, it has evolved into a thriving mixed community serving travelers from every country. The area features:

  • World-class entertainment: Nana Plaza, international bars, and nightlife venues
  • Global dining: Thai, Indian, Arabic, Japanese, Korean, Western restaurants
  • 24-hour activity: Unlike many Bangkok neighborhoods, Nana never sleeps
  • English-speaking services: Hotels, restaurants, and shops accustomed to foreign visitors
  • Excellent transport: BTS Nana gives instant access to the entire city
  • Diverse accommodation: Budget guesthouses to 4-star hotels within walking distance

For international travelers — especially solo male travelers and groups of friends — the Nana area represents the ideal base for exploring Bangkok. You're connected to the city by BTS, you're near the entertainment district, and you're surrounded by international infrastructure that makes travel easy.

Nana Area Geography: Understanding the Layout

Understanding Nana's geography helps you choose the right hotel location within the area:

The Core: Sukhumvit Soi 4 (Nana Tai)

Sukhumvit Soi 4 is the heart of Nana for international visitors. This is where you find Nana Plaza entertainment complex, Royal Ivory Nana Hotel, and a concentration of bars and restaurants. It's quieter than the main Sukhumvit Road but within 50 meters of it — ideal balance of access and calm.

The Strip: Main Sukhumvit Road (Sois 4-11)

The main Sukhumvit Road between Sois 4 and 11 is the busiest part of Nana. Hotels here have maximum access but maximum noise. Constant traffic, tuk-tuks, and street vendors operate 24 hours. Good for those who want to be in the absolute center of activity.

The Quiet Side: Soi 4 Side Streets

The side streets off Soi 4 — where Royal Ivory Nana is located — offer the best balance. You're 200 meters from Nana Plaza and 50 meters from main Sukhumvit Road, but on a quieter side street that allows genuine rest. This is the most recommended location type for travelers who want both access and sleep quality.

Location Recommendation

For the best Nana Bangkok hotel experience, choose a hotel on Sukhumvit Soi 4 or immediate side streets — not on the main Sukhumvit Road. You get easy walking access to everything without the main road noise pollution. Royal Ivory Nana at 73 Sukhumvit Soi 4 is the prime example of this optimal positioning.

What to Expect from Nana Bangkok Hotels

Hotels in the Nana area vary significantly in quality, policy, and value. Here's what you need to know before booking:

Joiner Charge Policies

One of the most important considerations when booking a Nana Bangkok hotel is the joiner charge policy. A joiner charge (also called a visitor fee or overnight guest fee) is an additional nightly charge for bringing a guest to your room.

  • Many Nana hotels charge 500–1,000 THB joiner fees
  • Some hotels have complete bans on outside visitors
  • Royal Ivory Nana Hotel has NO joiner charge — ever
  • Always confirm joiner policy before booking, as it's rarely listed on OTA sites

Room Sizes

Nana area hotel rooms vary dramatically:

  • Budget guesthouses: 12–20 sqm — very small, basic facilities
  • Standard 3-star: 20–28 sqm — adequate but not spacious
  • Royal Ivory Nana: 32-45 sqm — significantly above average

Noise Levels

The Nana area is active 24 hours. Hotels on the main Sukhumvit Road experience constant traffic noise. Hotels on the Soi 4 side streets (like Royal Ivory Nana) experience significantly less noise. For quality sleep, location within the Nana area matters enormously.

BTS Access

BTS Nana Station is on the main Sukhumvit Road. Walking time to BTS varies from 1 minute (hotels directly at the intersection) to 15+ minutes (hotels deeper in the sois). Royal Ivory Nana's 7-10 minute walk to BTS Nana (700m) is among the best in the area.

What to Do in the Nana Area: Complete Activity Guide

Evening & Nightlife

  • Nana Plaza: 4-story entertainment complex, 200m from Royal Ivory Nana — Bangkok's most famous international nightlife destination
  • Soi 4 bars: Multiple bars and restaurants directly on Soi 4, walkable from the hotel
  • Soi Cowboy: 15 minutes via BTS (2 stops to Asok) — another famous entertainment street
  • RCA District: Bangkok's clubbing strip, 30 minutes by taxi
  • Sukhumvit nightlife corridor: Bars and clubs between Sois 11-23

Daytime Activities

  • Terminal 21: Airport-themed mall, 7 minutes via BTS — excellent food court
  • Benjakitti Park: Lakeside park, 10 minutes by BTS for morning exercise
  • Grand Palace / Wat Pho: 45 minutes via BTS and Chao Phraya River boat
  • Chatuchak Weekend Market: 30 minutes via BTS — open Sat-Sun
  • Siam shopping district: 15 minutes via BTS — largest malls in Bangkok

Local Dining Near Nana

  • Indian restaurants: Concentrated on Sukhumvit Soi 11 (10 min walk)
  • Arabic restaurants: Soi 3 area (5 min walk)
  • Thai street food: Throughout Soi 4 and Sukhumvit Road
  • Japanese restaurants: Multiple options on Soi 6-8
  • Royal Ivory Restaurant: Thai and international cuisine in the hotel

Getting Around Bangkok from Nana

One of the Nana area's greatest advantages is connectivity. BTS Nana Station is the hub:

BTS Skytrain (Most Recommended)

  • BTS Nana Station: 2 minutes walk from Royal Ivory Nana Hotel
  • Asok (Terminal 21): 1 stop, 3 minutes
  • Siam (Shopping District): 4 stops, 12 minutes
  • Mo Chit (Chatuchak): 9 stops, 25 minutes
  • Mo Chit for Chatuchak Market: 30 minutes total
  • BTS runs 6:00 AM – midnight daily

MRT Connection

  • Asok BTS connects to Sukhumvit MRT (interchange)
  • MRT extends to Silom, Chinatown, Huamark Stadium
  • MRT runs 6:00 AM – midnight daily

Taxi & Grab

  • Grab app recommended for metered, predictable pricing
  • Taxis abundant in Nana area throughout the day and night
  • Royal Ivory Nana provides free tuk-tuk shuttle to BTS Nana

Frequently Asked Questions — Nana Bangkok Hotel Guide

What does "Nana Bangkok hotel" mean?

A "Nana Bangkok hotel" is a hotel located in the Nana district of Bangkok — the area surrounding Sukhumvit Sois 4–11 near BTS Nana Station. It's one of Bangkok's most internationally popular neighborhoods for travelers seeking nightlife, diverse dining, and convenient transport access.

Where exactly is the Nana area in Bangkok?

The Nana area is in the Sukhumvit district of Bangkok, accessible via BTS Nana Station on the Sukhumvit BTS line. It's between Asok (east) and Phloenchit (west) on the BTS map. By road, it's the section of Sukhumvit Road between Sois 4 and 11. About 4 km from Siam Square and 5 km from Silom.

Is the Nana area good for first-time Bangkok visitors?

Yes. The Nana area is excellent for first-time Bangkok visitors. English is widely spoken, services are internationally oriented, BTS access is easy, and the area has everything within walking distance. It's more manageable than some other tourist areas and provides a good introduction to Bangkok's international side. Many first-time visitors use it as a base for exploring the whole city via BTS.

How do I choose the right Nana Bangkok hotel?

Key factors when choosing a Nana Bangkok hotel: (1) Joiner charge policy — confirm before booking; (2) Room size — look for 30+ sqm for comfort; (3) Location within Nana — side streets quieter than main road; (4) BTS distance — aim for under 5 minutes; (5) Guest reviews — check recent reviews on multiple platforms; (6) What's included — breakfast, WiFi, pool.
